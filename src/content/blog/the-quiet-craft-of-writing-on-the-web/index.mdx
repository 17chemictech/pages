Not every site needs to act like an archive. Some pages are there to orient, tempt, sort, and pull readers toward the things they did not know they wanted to open.

## A front page is a filter

The archive keeps everything. The front page does the opposite. It selects, frames, and sequences. That is why `pages.olivie.space` exists as a separate layer: it is allowed to feel more visual, more selective, and more immediate than the long-term blog.

The best editorial homepages behave a little like a well-run shop. They do not throw the whole inventory at the door. They make a few things legible, desirable, and easy to enter.

## Discovery needs styling

People rarely subscribe because they respect your information architecture. They subscribe because something caught them at the right level first: a strong headline, a clear category, a good image, a sense of point of view.

This page should do that catching. It should feel lighter than a full essay site and more deliberate than a feed. That middle space is where curiosity becomes commitment.

## The handoff matters

The job is not to finish the whole relationship in one visit. The job is to hand readers somewhere better:

- to the blog when they want depth
- to the newsletter when they want recurrence
- to the main site when they want context

That is enough. A good front page earns the next click, not the last word.
