Traffic is useful. Relationship is better. That is the simplest reason to separate the public page from the newsletter.

## Discovery is fragile

People arrive from search, a forwarded link, a social post, or a passing recommendation. Most of them leave just as fast. The page layer exists to make that first encounter feel sharp and intentional, but it cannot create a lasting habit on its own.

## Email keeps the cadence

The newsletter is the place where taste turns into rhythm. A letter can carry context, tone, and sequence in a way a homepage cannot. It lets you say: here is what mattered this week, here is why it matters, here is what to read next.

That is why Quaily should not just mirror the site. It should compress and reinterpret it.

## The split keeps each surface honest

When one surface has to do every job, it becomes muddy. When each one has a clear role, it becomes easier to write for it.

- `pages` attracts attention
- `blog` holds the archive
- `newsletter` keeps the loop alive

Good systems are often just clear separations that remove confusion.
