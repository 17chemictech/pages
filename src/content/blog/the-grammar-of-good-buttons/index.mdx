Call-to-action language is where a multi-site setup either becomes clear or collapses into mush.

## Every surface has a next step

On `pages.olivie.space`, the button should sound like a curated invitation: start here, browse picks, join the list.

On `blog.olivie.space`, the button should sound archival: read the full post, see all essays, continue reading.

On the newsletter, the button should sound relational: join the list, get the next letter, receive weekly picks.

## The wrong CTA creates role confusion

If every button says `subscribe`, then the site starts feeling like a funnel and nothing else. If every button says `read more`, the newsletter loses its purpose.

## Good microcopy protects architecture

Short labels carry structural meaning:

- `Browse the archive` means depth
- `Join the list` means recurrence
- `Start with the lead story` means orientation

When the labels are right, the system feels easy without being explained.
