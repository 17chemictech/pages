You can learn more from twenty careful homepage edits than from one oversized strategy deck.

## Naming shows the real model

The first clue is labels. The moment categories become easier to name, the site model gets clearer. If you know the difference between `Features`, `Dispatches`, and `Conversations`, you are usually close to a usable structure.

## Spacing exposes uncertainty

Crowded cards and hesitant headings are rarely visual bugs alone. They usually mean the page is trying to do too many things at once. Removing one block often improves more than redesigning five.

## Repetition sharpens taste

The longer you work on one publishing surface, the more obvious certain standards become: stronger buttons, shorter subheads, clearer lanes, fewer decorative ideas.

Good taste is often just repeated editing with better judgment each round.
