Retail-influenced layouts are seductive because they know how to make things feel lively. They are risky because a lively page can quickly become a loud one.

## Type is the brake pedal

The solution is not to remove energy. It is to let typography carry the discipline. A page can use soft gradients, cards, color blocks, and merchandised sections as long as the type keeps the eye calm and the hierarchy obvious.

## Headings should sell, not shout

The best homepage headings do not behave like ads. They behave like invitations. They carry enough character to stop the scroll, but enough clarity to make the next click easy.

## Restraint is visible

You can usually tell when a page has discipline by checking three things:

- how many font sizes appear above the fold
- whether cards are allowed to breathe
- whether the button copy says exactly what happens next

Bright does not mean busy. A clean page earns its polish through limits.
