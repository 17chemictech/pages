We kept returning to the same question: why do some content pages feel collected while others just feel populated?

## Selection is visible

A curated page shows evidence of refusal. It does not display everything at once. It commits to a lead story, a supporting shelf, and a few carefully framed paths.

## Contrast carries confidence

Soft backgrounds and rounded cards are not enough. You need a hierarchy strong enough to make one thing obviously first, another thing obviously second, and the rest pleasantly browsable.

## Good curation implies return

The best compliment for a page like this is not `beautiful`. It is `I want to come back and see what changed`.

That is when curation becomes habit, not just decoration.
