The easiest mistake in personal publishing is to let every surface publish everything. It feels productive for a week and chaotic for a year.

## Surfaces are jobs

When I separate `www`, `blog`, `pages`, and `letters`, I am not multiplying websites for the sake of it. I am assigning jobs:

- one domain introduces
- one domain archives
- one domain attracts
- one channel retains

That structure makes the writing workflow simpler, not heavier.

## Duplication is a maintenance bug

If the same article appears in full on three surfaces, then metadata, links, revisions, and analytics all become harder to trust. You also teach readers that any entry point is interchangeable, which makes brand memory weaker instead of stronger.

## Build for handoff

The better pattern is:

- publish the full piece once
- summarize or frame it elsewhere
- use each surface to move readers toward the next one

That kind of system disappears in use, which is exactly what a good publishing stack should do.
